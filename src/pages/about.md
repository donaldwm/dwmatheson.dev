## Professional Life

My name is Donald Matheson and I am a Software Architect by day. While I don't build things as much as I used to in the day job, I still get the opportunity occasionally and have always enjoyed learning and figuring things out in my own time.

I think like everyone else in the industry at the moment, trying to get my head around AI and how it can aid me personally, and the dev teams at work in what we do. I may post about my findings here.

During my career I've progressed through various technology stacks. Starting out with 4GLs in business applications running on various forms of Unix and Windows, a lttle but of Windows development, through to web development starting out with Classic ASP and now mainly working with C# and JavaScript (in a variety of frameworks) along with MS SQL. I have always worked on the full stack, sure my design skills may not be the best at times, but in terms of the tech I can work at all levels.

I've done most things in tech having, until now, worked for smaller tech companies. Started out on a support and development role, which meant dealing with the calls as well as actually fixing them, no second line back up for us. Handled the analysis and design of features to be implemented, lost of pre and post sales demos and creating and delivering training to both technical developers and end users, followed by getting those systems up and running for customers. I've even had a Product Manager role. I've lead teams of varying sizes mentoring team members while also being a contributing member of the team.

## Personal Life

I'm currently getting back into retro development. I got my first computer back in 1985, an Acorn Electron. I learnt Basic on the Electron, before I started to look at 6502. I then progressed to an Atari ST, closely followed by an Amiga A1200, before I got my first PC. The ST and A1200 saw me through University, especially useful as we covered 68000 while there. While I run Windows mainly at the moment, I first discovered Linux around that time in Uni and have periodically dabbled over the years. We mainly used Unix in the labs when we weren't doing assembly so have always been comfortable in those environs.

Away from the computer I do love my music. I learnt to play guitar a long, long time ago and have threatened to get back into it. I've always loved going to gigs and while we had a hiatus while my son was younger, we are back on that horse and taking him to many of them too. I'm still one for physical media, whether on CD or vinyl and far prefer this over the streaming services.

I do also love photography and while I have a link in the footer to my [Flickr](https://www.flickr.com/photos/hairyhighlander/) site, it is woefully neglected, something I am planning to address in the coming months as I get back out there with my camera.

As well as coaching football, I also support Ross County and have followed them through the ups and downs since the early 80s when they were still in the Highland League.

## Coaching Life

By night (well three nights a week and on a Sunday morning) I am a grassroots football coach. I am the head coach of my son's team and have been coaching since 2017. I also run separate Goalkeeping sessions for the wider community club that my son's team is a part of, so work with a variety of ages of boys and girls either starting out on their goalkeeping journey or having been doing it for a few years. At times this is like a second job, but it is extremely rewarding from a personal perspective.

## About this website

In the past I used Wordpress for my blog, but when I decided I wanted to start this again I didn't want the hassle of dealing with upgrading, and security issues so wanted to head down the static site route. Rather than re-invent the wheel and build something myself I settled on [11ty](https://www.11ty.dev/). After a bit of research I happened upon the [Eleventy Excellent](https://github.com/madrilene/eleventy-excellent) starter project by [Lene Saile](https://www.lenesaile.com/en/), and have used this as the basis for this site. It is also affording the opportunity to learn some front end tech that I haven't used much of before.
